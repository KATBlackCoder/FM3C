From **March 22 to 24, 2026**, the Acropole de Bamako hosted the first national hand-to-hand fighting competition organized under the banner of the Malian Federation of Hand-to-Hand Fighting — the first public milestone of a federation still being constituted, carried by its founding association.

## Three days of competition

Nearly **70 athletes**, from clubs across the country, competed for three days in the categories set out by the discipline's rules. For many of them, it was the first opportunity to represent their club in an official competition under the FMCCC's banner.

Refereeing followed the discipline's international rules, with the participation, as judge, of a Russian master of sport — a concrete illustration of the sports cooperation between Mali and Russia that has accompanied the project since its beginnings.

## A first milestone

This first edition marks the starting point of the FMCCC's sporting calendar: it helped fine-tune the organization, bring clubs together around a shared event, and introduce the discipline to a new audience in Bamako.

The FMCCC thanks the athletes, coaches, officials, and all the volunteers who made this first edition possible. **The next national competition is already in preparation** — it will be announced here and in the competition calendar.
