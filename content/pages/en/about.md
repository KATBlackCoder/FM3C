The **Malian Federation of Hand-to-Hand Fighting (FMCCC)** aims to become the national reference body for the practice of hand-to-hand fighting in Mali. The association carrying the project was founded in Bamako, and the federation's official constitution is underway. It already brings together the clubs that keep the discipline alive across the country, organizes national competitions, and will represent Mali before the discipline's international bodies.

## Our mission

- **Develop the discipline** in Mali, supporting the creation and affiliation of clubs in every region;
- **Organize national competitions** and ensure they run according to the discipline's international rules;
- **Train and coach** athletes, coaches, and referees, from first steps to elite level;
- **Promote sporting values** — respect, discipline, self-improvement — and the health benefits of practice;
- **Represent Malian hand-to-hand fighting** before national and international sporting bodies.

## The discipline

Hand-to-hand fighting is a comprehensive combat sport derived from Russian *rukopashny boy*: it combines controlled strikes, throws, holds, and submission techniques. Competitions take place in two disciplines: **Combat**, by weight category or open category, and **Self-defense**, where athletes demonstrate defense techniques against an armed or unarmed opponent.

The discipline is governed internationally by the HSIF (Hand-to-Hand Fighting Sport International Federation), whose competition rules have been in force since 2006. Practitioners' progression follows its international belt system, structuring the path from beginner to the highest ranks.

## Our history

The FMCCC project was born in March 2026 in Bamako, the culmination of work to structure the discipline in Mali: the association carrying it was founded, and the federation's official constitution is underway. As early as March 2026, the first national competition was organized, from March 22 to 24 at the Acropole de Bamako, bringing together nearly 70 athletes — with the participation, as judge, of a Russian master of sport, African champion of the discipline.

Since then, new clubs have progressively joined the association across the country, and the next national competition is in preparation.

## Mali-Russia cooperation

The FMCCC is part of the sports cooperation between Mali and Russia, carried by the association La Perspective Sahélienne. This cooperation translates into concrete exchanges: transmission of technical expertise for a discipline born in Russia, participation of Russian coaches in its events, and a link with the HSIF for the international recognition of Malian practice.

This is also the purpose of this site, published in three languages: French, English, and Russian.

## Our structure

The association is chaired by **Alioune Badara Traoré**, also technical director of the National Olympic and Sports Committee of Mali (CNOSM). The full composition of the board will be published on this page once the federation is officially constituted.
